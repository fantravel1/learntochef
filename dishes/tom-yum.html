Tom Yum

Thailand's most famous soup. Hot, sour, sweet, savory all in perfect balance. A lesson in flavor layering.

The Four Flavors

Tom Yum teaches balance. Heat (chili), sour (lime), sweet (palm sugar), and savory (fish sauce) must coexist. Taste constantly and adjust one element at a time.

üî• Heat

Thai chili paste or fresh chilis. Builds slowly, builds intensity.

üçã Sour

Lime juice. Brightness, acid, awakens the palate.

üçØ Sweet

Palm sugar. Rounds out heat, balances sour.

Quick Recipe (15 min)

Ingredients (Serves 4)

  • 4 cups chicken or seafood stock
  • 8 oz shrimp or chicken, sliced
  • 3 tbsp Thai chili paste
  • 3 tbsp fish sauce
  • 3 tbsp palm sugar
  • Juice of 3 limes
  • 3-4 stalks lemongrass, bruised
  • 2 tbsp galangal, sliced
  • 1 tbsp garlic, minced
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

The Technique

1
Bloom the Paste (2 min)
In a large pot, bring stock to boil. Add chili paste, whisk until dissolved. You're blooming the paste to release its flavor.
2
Infuse Aromatics (5 min)
Add lemongrass and galangal. Simmer to infuse. These essential oils take 5 minutes to transfer to the liquid.
3
Add Protein (3 min)
Add shrimp or chicken. Cook until just done. Shrimp takes 2 minutes, chicken 5.
4
Balance the Four Flavors (3 min)
Add fish sauce, palm sugar, and lime juice. Taste. Heat too strong? Add more lime. Not salty enough? More fish sauce. This is where you learn balance.
5
Serve Immediately
Garnish with cilantro. Serve hot.
