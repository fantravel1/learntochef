Speed & Balance

Pad Thai is not just a noodle dish ‚Äî it's a lesson in balance and speed. Every element must be prepared before you touch the wok. Everything cooks in 3 minutes. The sauce is the hero: sweet (palm sugar), sour (lime/tamarind), salty (fish sauce), spicy (chili) in perfect harmony.

üî• The 60-Second Rule

If your Pad Thai takes longer than 3 minutes once you hit the wok, something is wrong. Your wok isn't hot enough, your mise en place isn't ready, or you're overcrowding the wok.

The Recipe

Ingredients (Per Serving)

  • 8 oz rice noodles (pad thai noodles, ¬º" wide)
  • 2 oz protein (shrimp, chicken, tofu, or combo)
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup vegetables (bell pepper, green beans, carrots - julienned)
  • 3 tbsp bean sprouts
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-2 scallions, cut into 1" pieces
  • 2 tbsp peanuts, crushed

The Sauce (Make First!)

  • 3 tbsp tamarind paste (or lime juice)
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp palm sugar (or brown sugar)
  • 1 tsp chili flakes

Mix all together. Taste it. If too salty, add lime juice. If too sour, add sugar. This is your flavor profile ‚Äî get it right before you cook.

3-Minute Cooking Sequence

0:00
Prep the Noodles
Soak rice noodles in room-temperature water for 30 minutes until pliable but still firm. Drain well. Have all mise en place ready before you touch the wok.
0:30
Get the Wok Smoking
Heat wok over high heat for 2-3 minutes until smoking hot. Add 2 tbsp peanut oil. Let heat for 10 seconds. Add minced garlic. The smell should be immediate.
0:45
Protein First (15 seconds)
Add protein. If shrimp, cook 1 minute per side. If chicken, cook 1-2 minutes. If tofu, just add with vegetables. Keep moving.
1:00
Crack Egg & Add Noodles (30 seconds)
Push protein to the side. Crack egg into the wok. Scramble lightly. Add noodles and vegetables. Toss constantly. Everything should be moving.
1:30
Pour Sauce (30 seconds)
Pour the entire sauce over the noodles. Toss rapidly. Every piece of noodle should be coated. The sauce should coat but not pool.
2:00
Add Aromatics & Plate (60 seconds)
Add scallions and bean sprouts. Toss once. Transfer to plate immediately. Top with crushed peanuts and lime wedge. Serve immediately.

‚è±Ô∏è Timing is Everything

Too long in the wok? Your noodles become mushy. Speed is the point.

Cold wok? You're steaming, not stir-frying. The wok must be smoking.

Sauce not coating? You need more heat. The wok cooled down. This is the most common mistake.
