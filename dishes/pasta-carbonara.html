Pasta Carbonara

Creamy pasta without cream. One of the most elegant lessons in emulsification and temperature control.

Emulsification Without Cream

Carbonara proves that creaminess doesn't require cream. The egg yolks, fat from guanciale, and starch from pasta water combine to create an emulsion. Miss the temperature by 5 degrees and the eggs scramble. Perfect it, and you have silk.

The Recipe (Serves 2)

Ingredients

  • 8 oz spaghetti or guanciale pasta
  • 4 oz guanciale (or pancetta), cut into ¬º" pieces
  • 3 egg yolks (room temperature)
  • 1 oz Pecorino Romano, grated
  • Fresh cracked black pepper
  • Salt

The 7-Minute Technique

1
Prep Eggs (2 min before pasta)
Combine egg yolks with grated Pecorino and lots of cracked pepper in a bowl. Whisk lightly. Set at room temperature.
2
Cook Guanciale (5 min)
In a large pan over medium heat, cook guanciale until fat renders and edges are crispy. Don't fully cook ‚Äî you want some chew. Lower heat to minimum.
3
Cook Pasta (9 min)
Bring salted water to boil. Cook pasta 1 minute LESS than package instructions (it should be slightly underdone). You're not done cooking it yet.
4
Combine (2 min)
Reserve 1 cup pasta water. Drain pasta. Add hot pasta to guanciale pan (heat still on minimum). Toss constantly. The pasta water goes into the pan bit by bit while you toss.
5
Add Eggs (CRITICAL)
Remove pan from heat completely. Add egg mixture. Toss constantly for 30 seconds. The residual heat cooks the eggs into a creamy sauce. Add pasta water as needed for creaminess.
6
Plate Immediately
Serve in warm bowls. Do not hold. Carbonara waits for no one.

‚ö†Ô∏è Temperature is Everything

If the pan is too hot when you add the eggs, they'll scramble. If it's too cold, they won't emulsify. Room temperature eggs + residual heat from pasta + constant tossing = creamy sauce. It's a three-degree dance.
