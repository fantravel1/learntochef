Bouillabaisse

The legendary seafood stew of Provence. Multiple fish, saffron, and rouille. A masterclass in building complex broths from seafood trimmings.

The Marseille Treasure

Bouillabaisse is not a simple fish stew. It's a complete lesson in French cooking: building stock from fish trimmings, infusing with saffron and fennel, preparing multiple fish varieties, and creating rouille (saffron aioli). Mastering this dish unlocks dozens of other seafood-based classics.

Key Components

Every element matters in this complex dish

The Broth

  • Fish heads and bones from 3-4 lbs mixed fish
  • Saffron (1 tsp quality threads)
  • Fennel bulb, sliced
  • Orange zest
  • Garlic cloves (6-8)
  • Onions, tomatoes

The Fish Selection

  • Monkfish (firm, meaty)
  • Sea bass or snapper (delicate)
  • Mackerel or john dory (oily)
  • Shellfish (mussels, clams, lobster)

The Rouille & Accompaniments

  • Garlic cloves (4-6)
  • Saffron (pinch)
  • Mayonnaise base or egg yolk
  • Best olive oil (Proven√ßal)
  • Crusty bread (toasted)

The Three-Hour Process

1
Build the Stock (45 min)
Saut√© aromatic vegetables. Add fish heads and bones. Cover with water. Simmer 30 minutes. Strain. This is your foundation ‚Äî quality stock makes or breaks bouillabaisse.
2
Infuse with Saffron (10 min)
Toast saffron threads. Steep in hot stock. Add fennel, orange zest. This is where the signature Proven√ßal flavor develops.
3
Prepare Rouille (10 min)
Pound garlic and saffron. Add egg yolk or mayo. Slowly whisk in olive oil until emulsified. This is the golden garnish that defines bouillabaisse.
4
Cook Fish (12-15 min)
Add firm fish first (monkfish). After 5 minutes, add delicate fish. Add shellfish last. The key: don't overcrowd or overstew. Each fish has a critical moment.
5
Finish & Serve
Ladle into bowls. Top with toasted bread, rouille, and fresh herbs. The presentation matters ‚Äî bouillabaisse is as much about theater as flavor.

Stock Quality: The entire dish rests on your stock. Don't rush it. 30 minutes minimum.

Fish Selection: You need variety: firm (monkfish), delicate (sea bass), and oily (mackerel). The complexity comes from the contrast.

Timing: Each fish type has a precise cooking time. Monkfish: 8 min. Sea bass: 5 min. The difference between silk and rubber.
