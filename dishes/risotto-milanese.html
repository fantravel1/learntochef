Risotto Milanese

Italian saffron rice made creamy through constant stirring and technique alone. Simplicity that requires mastery.

The Art of Constant Stirring

Risotto is not cooked risotto until you've stood at the stove for 18 minutes, constantly stirring. The stirring releases starch from the rice, which emulsifies with the liquid, creating a creamy sauce ‚Äî no cream needed. This teaches you about emulsification, a core cooking principle used in sauces worldwide.

Method

1. Toast rice: Heat butter in a pan. Add minced onion, cook 2 min. Add rice, stir 1 min.

2. Deglaze: Add wine. Stir until absorbed (2-3 min).

3. Add stock: Add warm stock 1 ladle at a time. Stir constantly. Wait until each ladle is mostly absorbed before adding more. 15-18 minutes total.

4. Finish: When rice is creamy and al dente, stir in butter and cheese. Serve immediately.
