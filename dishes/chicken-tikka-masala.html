Chicken Tikka Masala

Tandoori chicken in a creamy tomato sauce. Learn spice building, marinating technique, and how to create depth without a tandoor.

45 min
Serves 4

Spice Layering

Tikka Masala teaches you how to layer spices. You start with a spiced yogurt marinade, then bloom spices in hot oil, then build a sauce. Each step adds complexity. This is how Indian cooking achieves depth.

The Recipe

For the Marinade

  • 1 cup Greek yogurt
  • 2 tbsp garam masala
  • 1 tbsp paprika
  • 1 tbsp turmeric
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 lbs chicken, cut into chunks
  • Salt to taste

For the Sauce

  • 2 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Step-by-Step

1
Marinate (Minimum 2 hours, ideally overnight)
Mix yogurt, spices, lemon juice, and ginger-garlic paste. Add chicken, coat thoroughly. Refrigerate. The yogurt tenderizes and the spices penetrate. This is not optional.
2
Sear the Chicken
Heat ghee in a large pan over high heat. Sear marinated chicken until golden on all sides (3-4 minutes). Don't crowd the pan. Work in batches if needed. Remove and set aside.
3
Build the Sauce Base
In the same pan, saut√© diced onion until soft (5 minutes). Add ginger-garlic paste, cook 1 minute. Add garam masala and turmeric, cook 30 seconds (blooming spices). Add tomato paste, cook 1 minute.
4
Add Tomatoes & Reduce
Add crushed tomatoes. Bring to boil, then simmer 10 minutes. The sauce should thicken and deepen in color. Taste and adjust spices.
5
Add Cream & Return Chicken
Pour in heavy cream and stir. Return chicken (and any juices) to the pan. Simmer 10 minutes until chicken is cooked through. The sauce should be rich and creamy.
6
Finish & Serve
Taste and adjust salt and spices. Serve over basmati rice with naan bread. Garnish with fresh cilantro.

üîë The Marinating Secret

Yogurt contains lactic acid, which tenderizes chicken like a brine. But it also adds flavor. Don't skip this step. If rushed, minimum 30 minutes, but 4+ hours is ideal.
