Peking Duck

Beijing's imperial dish. Crispy skin from air-drying, tender meat from precise roasting. A masterclass in poultry technique.

The Beijing Technique

Peking Duck teaches you the science of rendering fat while crisping skin. The duck is air-dried for 24 hours, then roasted at high heat. The result: skin so crispy it shatters, meat that's impossibly tender. This is poultry mastery.

Watch: Peking Duck Roasting

The 48-Hour Process

Day 1 (Preparation): Clean duck thoroughly. Remove any pinfeathers. Blanch in boiling water to tighten skin. Hang in cool, dry place for 24 hours for air-drying.

Day 2 (Roasting): Brush with glaze (honey, soy, vinegar). Roast at 400¬∞F for 1.5 hours, rotating halfway. The skin should be deep mahogany and crackle when tapped.

Serving: Carve skin and meat separately. Serve with thin pancakes, hoisin sauce, and julienned vegetables. Each bite is a perfect balance.
