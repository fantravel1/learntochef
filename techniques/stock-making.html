Liquid gold. The foundation of French cooking. 4-24 hours of simmering bones, aromatics, and patience.

The Three Stocks

White Stock

Chicken bones, not browned. Clear, delicate. Used for velout√© and light soups. 4-6 hours simmering.

Brown Stock

Beef bones, browned in oven. Rich, dark. Used for espagnole, demi-glace. 8-24 hours simmering.

Fish Fumet

Fish bones, white wine. Delicate. Used for seafood sauces. Only 30-45 minutes simmering.

Brown Stock Recipe (The Workhorse)

Ingredients

5 lbs beef bones, 2 tbsp tomato paste, 1 lb mirepoix (onion, carrot, celery), 3 cloves garlic, water, bouquet garni (thyme, bay, parsley)

Step 1: Brown the Bones (1 hour)

Place bones on sheet pan. Roast at 400¬∞F until deep brown (not black). Transfer to large pot. The browning creates the deep flavor.

Step 2: Brown the Vegetables (15 min)

In same pan with remaining fat, saut√© mirepoix until brown. Add tomato paste, cook 2 minutes. Transfer to pot.

Step 3: Deglaze (5 min)

Add water to the roasting pan, scrape up all browned bits. Pour into pot. Cover bones with water (about 1 gallon).

Step 4: Simmer Low & Slow (12-24 hours)

Bring to bare simmer. Skim scum that rises. Maintain gentle simmer. The longer it cooks, the more collagen becomes gelatin, the more rich the stock becomes.

Step 5: Strain & Cool

Strain through fine sieve. Cool completely. Fat will solidify on top ‚Äî remove or leave for cooking. Refrigerate or freeze.
