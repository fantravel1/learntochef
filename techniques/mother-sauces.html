Why These Five Sauces Matter

The five mother sauces were codified by the great French chef Escoffier as the foundation of French classical cooking. Every sauce in Western cuisine ‚Äî whether French, Italian, American, or modern ‚Äî derives from one of these five. Master them, and you can make any sauce in the world.

Understanding Roux

The foundation of mother sauces (except Hollandaise)

White Roux

1:1 ratio of butter and flour. Cook briefly (1-2 minutes) until the raw flour taste disappears but the roux stays pale. Used for B√©chamel.

Blonde Roux

Cook longer (3-5 minutes) until golden but not brown. The deeper color provides more flavor. Used for Velout√©.

Brown Roux

Cook 10-15 minutes until deep chocolate brown. Requires constant stirring to prevent burning. Used for Espagnole.

üî• Roux Mastery Tips

  • Always cook the roux BEFORE adding liquid ‚Äî never add cold liquid to raw roux
  • Let the roux cool slightly before whisking in liquid to prevent lumps
  • Whisk constantly when adding liquid to prevent lumps from forming
  • A good roux should be a smooth paste, not too thick and not too thin
  • The flavor deepens as roux cooks longer, but don't burn it

The Five Mother Sauces

B√©chamel

White Sauce

Recipe

  • 2 oz butter
  • 2 oz flour
  • 1 pint whole milk
  • Salt, white pepper, nutmeg

Method: Make white roux with butter and flour. Warm milk separately. Whisk milk into roux slowly to prevent lumps. Simmer 10 minutes while stirring. Season.

Derivatives: Mornay (add cheese), Soubise (add onion pur√©e), Mustard sauce.

Velout√©

Blonde Sauce

Recipe

  • 2 oz butter
  • 2 oz flour
  • 1 pint chicken/fish/veal stock
  • Salt and pepper

Method: Make blonde roux. Heat stock. Whisk stock into roux slowly. Simmer 10 minutes. Strain through fine sieve. Season.

Derivatives: Allemande (add egg yolk and cream), Poulette (add mushrooms and cream), Ravigote (add herbs and vinegar).

Espagnole

Brown Sauce

Recipe

  • 3 oz butter or oil
  • 3 oz flour
  • 1 quart brown stock
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • Mirepoix (2 oz each)

Method: Make dark brown roux. Add tomato paste, cook 2 minutes. Whisk in brown stock slowly. Add mirepoix. Simmer 1 hour, strain.

Derivatives: Demi-glace (reduced espagnole), Bordelaise (add red wine), Poivrade (add pepper).

Hollandaise

Emulsion Sauce

Recipe

  • 3 egg yolks
  • 6 oz clarified butter
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • Water (2-3 tbsp)
  • Salt, cayenne

Method: Whisk egg yolks with water over double boiler until pale. Slowly whisk in warm clarified butter. Remove from heat. Add lemon juice. Season.

Derivatives: B√©arnaise (add tarragon vinegar reduction), Maltaise (add orange juice), Mousseline (add whipped cream).

Tomato Sauce

Red Sauce

Recipe

  • 2 lbs fresh or canned tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small onion, minced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Herbs (basil, oregano)

Method: Saut√© onion and garlic in oil. Add tomatoes and herbs. Simmer 30 minutes. Pass through food mill for smooth sauce. Season.

Derivatives: Arrabbiata (add chili), Marinara (simplest version), Puttanesca (add olives and capers).

Sauce Mastery Fundamentals

‚úì The Five Fundamentals

  • Taste constantly. Sauce development is about balance. Taste at every stage.
  • Strain for smoothness. Always pass sauces through a fine sieve for restaurant quality.
  • Finish with acid and fat. A squeeze of lemon or knob of butter transforms a good sauce into a great one.
  • Temperature matters. Hollandaise breaks if too hot. Brown sauce needs time to reduce and concentrate.
  • Emulsions require patience. Whisk slowly and steadily. If your hollandaise breaks, start over with a fresh egg yolk and whisk in the broken sauce slowly.
