Plating & Presentation

The final touch. Color, height, balance, white space. A beautiful plate brings the entire dish together.

The Five Principles

üé® Color & Contrast

Use contrasting colors. Red against green. White against dark. Monochromatic plates are boring. Vary the palette.

üìè Height & Dimension

Flat plates are forgettable. Stack, lean, angle. Create vertical interest. The eye travels up and down, not just left to right.

‚öñÔ∏è Balance & Composition

Imagine the plate divided into thirds (vertical and horizontal). Place your focal point at an intersection, not dead center. This is the "rule of thirds" from photography.

ü§ç White Space

Less is more. Don't fill every inch. Negative space gives the eye rest and makes the food stand out. A cluttered plate looks chaotic.

‚ú® Clean Edges

Wipe drips before serving. A smudged plate edge says carelessness. Use a clean cloth or squeeze bottle with care. Details matter.
