Braising

Low-temperature cooking that transforms tough cuts into silk. Brown, braise in liquid, reduce sauce. The foundation of comfort food.

The Three Stages

Stage 1: Brown (Maillard Reaction)

Sear meat on all sides in hot fat. This creates a flavorful crust. Don't skip this step â€” it's where deep flavor comes from.

Stage 2: Braise (Low & Slow)

Add liquid (wine, stock, or combination). Cover. Cook low and slow in oven at 300-325Â°F. Collagen breaks down into gelatin. The meat becomes tender.

Stage 3: Reduce (Sauce Building)

Remove meat. Reduce braising liquid on stovetop until it becomes sauce. Strain if needed. The concentrated liquid coats the meat.
