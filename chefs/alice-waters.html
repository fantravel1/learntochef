Alice Waters

The mother of farm-to-table. Founder of Chez Panisse. Changed how America thinks about food, sourcing, and seasonality.

The Philosopher of Food

Alice Waters opened Chez Panisse in 1971 with a radical idea: let the ingredient speak. She built relationships with farmers. She changed the menu based on what was in season. She proved that respecting ingredients and their origins made better food than imported luxuries.

Her Philosophy: "Cooking Is a Political Act"
