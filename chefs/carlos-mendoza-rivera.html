The Story

Carlos grew up in rural Argentina, where his family's asado traditions were passed down through generations. His early understanding of open-fire cooking came from family gatherings in the Pampas, where fire mastery was essential to survival and celebration.

He expanded his knowledge throughout Latin America, learning mole preparation in Oaxaca, ceviche techniques in Peru, and tropical cooking in Brazil. His restaurant in Buenos Aires earned one Michelin star for its focus on heritage techniques and sustainable, locally-sourced ingredients.

Today, Carlos is dedicated to documenting endangered cooking techniques before they disappear, and teaching the world that Latin American cuisine is far more sophisticated and diverse than commonly understood.
