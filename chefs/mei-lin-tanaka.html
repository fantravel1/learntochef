The Story

Mei Lin Tanaka's journey began in a Taipei wet market with her grandmother, where she learned to identify the freshest ingredients and understand the balance of flavors. At age 18, she moved to Tokyo to train under a master wok chef, spending 10 years perfecting high-heat cooking and understanding the science of umami.

Her breakthrough came when she earned two Michelin stars for her Tokyo restaurant, which focused on the intersection of Japanese precision and Chinese wok mastery. Her research into umami compounds and their applications has influenced contemporary cooking worldwide.

Her Technique Signature

Wok Mastery

High heat control, proper wrist motion, understanding the breath of the flame. Every motion is deliberate and efficient.

Umami Understanding

Mastery of glutamates, nucleotides, and flavor layering. Knows exactly which ingredients to combine for maximum depth.

Knife Skills

Precise vegetable cuts optimized for heat penetration and textural consistency in high-speed stir-fries.

Heat Philosophy

Understanding that Asian cooking is about fire control ‚Äî knowing when to blast and when to coast.

Flavor Balance

Stock Building

Cook Like Mei Lin ‚Äî The Learning Path

Stage 1: Understanding Fire & Umami (Weeks 1-4)

  • Wok fundamentals: carbon steel seasoning and maintenance
  • Understanding the flames: breath of heat, hot zones, cooling zones
  • The five basic tastes and their balance
  • Introduction to umami compounds
  • Knife skills for high-speed stir-fry
  • Building your first simple stir-fry

Stage 2: Mastering Dashi & Broths (Weeks 5-8)

  • Kombu (seaweed) and bonito dashi
  • Vegetarian dashi variations
  • Chicken and seafood broths
  • Building flavor layers in broths
  • Understanding glutamates and nucleotides
  • Stock timing and extraction

Stage 3: Wok Mastery (Weeks 9-12)

  • High-heat stir-fry techniques
  • Building complex flavors in 60 seconds
  • Vegetable stir-fries with perfect texture
  • Protein cooking in a wok
  • Sauce building while cooking
  • The art of "wok breath" and timing

Stage 4: Thai & Southeast Asian Specialties (Weeks 13-16)

  • Curry paste making and balancing
  • Green, red, and yellow curries
  • Tom yum soup and balance techniques
  • Pad thai and street noodle dishes
  • Southeast Asian umami combinations
  • Heat control for Southeast Asian cooking

Stage 5: Advanced Sichuan & Professional Mastery (Weeks 17-20)

  • Sichuan peppercorn and numbing sensation
  • Building Sichuan flavor bases
  • Mapo tofu and advanced Sichuan dishes
  • Chili oils and infusions
  • Multi-component Asian dishes
  • Restaurant-level execution and plating
