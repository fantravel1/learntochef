The golden foundation of French cooking. From hollandaise to beurre blanc, butter is emulsification itself.

Butter Types

American Butter

80% Butterfat

What it is: Standard commercial butter. ~80% fat, 15-16% water, 4% milk solids.

Use for: Cooking, baking, most applications. Neutral flavor.

European Butter

86% Butterfat

What it is: Higher butterfat (86%). Less water = richer, more flavorful.

Use for: Finishing, pastries, sauces where butter flavor shines.

Cultured Butter

84-86% Butterfat

What it is: Fermented cream. Adds tang and complexity. More expensive.

Use for: Premium pastries, finishing sauces, when butter is the star.

Clarified Butter (Ghee)

99% Pure Fat

What it is: Pure butterfat. Water and milk solids removed. 450¬∞F smoke point.

Use for: High-heat cooking, hollandaise base, Indian cooking.

How to Clarify Butter

The Method

1. Melt: Cut butter into chunks. Melt gently on low heat. Don't stir. The milk solids will sink to the bottom, fat floats to the top.

2. Skim: Carefully pour off the liquid (pure butterfat) into a container, leaving the solids in the pan.

3. Cool & Store: Clarified butter solidifies as it cools. Store in the fridge. Lasts 6+ months.

Why Clarify?

Pure fat has a higher smoke point (450¬∞F vs 350¬∞F for whole butter). This means you can use it for high-heat cooking. The milk solids burn easily, so removing them prevents browning and burning.
