The Grades of Olive Oil

Olive oil is graded by acidity level and processing method. Higher grades have lower acidity and more complex flavors. The trade-off: premium oils are delicate and shouldn't be heated above their smoke point.

How to Buy & Store

üõí Selection Tips

Look for "cold-pressed": This means no heat was used. Higher quality.

Check the harvest date: Olive oil is best within 2 years of harvest. Older = less flavorful.

Dark bottle matters: Light damages olive oil. Dark glass protects it.

Taste it first: Buy small bottles from specialty shops that let you taste before buying.

Price reflects quality: EVOO should cost $15-40/bottle. Cheaper = likely inferior quality or blended.

üè∫ Storage

Cool, dark place: Pantry is ideal. Avoid direct sunlight.

Not the refrigerator: Cold temperatures cause cloudiness (it's fine, just cosmetic).

Shelf life: 18-24 months for EVOO, longer for refined oils.

After opening: Use within 6 months. Exposure to air causes oxidation.
