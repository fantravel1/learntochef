The foundation vegetable. In every cuisine, every stock, every sauce. Caramelization transforms them from pungent to deeply sweet.

Onion Varieties

Yellow Onions

Sweet when cooked, pungent when raw. The workhorse. Use for mirepoix, sauces, caramelizing. Most versatile.

Red Onions

Purple flesh, milder, slightly sweet. Use for salads, salsas, pickling. Won't caramelize as deeply as yellow.

White Onions

Sharper flavor. Mexican and Asian cuisines. Pungent raw, sweet cooked. Less common in French cooking.

Sweet Onions (Vidalia, Maui)

Lower sulfur content = sweeter. Use raw in salads or for special applications. Caramelize beautifully.

Caramelization vs Sweating

Sweating (5-10 min)

Medium heat, covered with butter or oil. Onions release water and become translucent and soft. The starting point for soups and sauces. Not brown.

Caramelization (30-45 min)

Medium heat, uncovered, patience. The natural sugars break down and recombine, turning golden then brown. Deep, complex, sweet flavor. This is magic.

The Caramelization Secret

Don't Rush. Low to medium heat. Let them sit for long periods without stirring. When they start to brown, stir. This prevents burning while allowing deep color to develop. 45 minutes for true caramelization.
