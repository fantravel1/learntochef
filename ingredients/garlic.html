How Temperature Changes Flavor

Raw Garlic

Flavor: Sharp, pungent, spicy. Sulfur compounds intact.

Use for: Vinaigrettes, mayonnaise, salsa, pesto. Raw applications only.

Quick-Saut√©ed (30 seconds)

Flavor: Sharp becomes aromatic. Heat partially breaks sulfur compounds.

Use for: Base for soups, stir-fries, most savory dishes.

Medium-Cooked (2-3 minutes)

Flavor: Mellow, slightly sweet. Caramelization begins.

Use for: Pasta sauces, braised dishes, Coq au Vin bases.

Roasted (40 minutes @ 400¬∞F)

Flavor: Sweet, nutty, almost mild. Completely transformed.

Use for: Spreads, mashed potatoes, finishing vegetables.

Burned Garlic

Flavor: Bitter, acrid, unpleasant.

Use for: Discard and start over. Burned garlic ruins a dish.

Selection & Storage

üõí What to Look For

Firm heads: Press gently. Should feel solid, not soft or spongy.

No sprouts: Green shoots mean old garlic (flavor diminished).

Papery skin: Dry outer layers are good. They protect the cloves.

No mold: Black or blue spots = mold. Don't buy.

üè∫ Storage

Cool, dry place: Pantry is ideal. Never refrigerate whole heads.

Shelf life: 2-3 weeks if kept cool and dry. Longer if stored properly.

Once cut: Use immediately or store in refrigerator in airtight container (3-4 days).

üö´ Don't Use Pre-Minced

Pre-minced garlic in jars loses potency quickly. The flavor oxidizes. Always mince fresh garlic when possible.
