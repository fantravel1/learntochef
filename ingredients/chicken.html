Chicken Types

Industrial/Commodity

Raised 6-7 weeks. Large breasts, small thighs. Mild flavor but less developed.

Best for: Mild dishes, breading, quick cooking.

Free-Range/Pasture

Raised 8-12 weeks with outdoor access. More movement = more flavorful, tougher texture.

Best for: Braising, roasting, long cooking.

Heritage Breeds

Slow-growing (16+ weeks). Exceptional flavor and texture. Expensive but worth it.

Best for: Slow roasting, stock, special occasions.

Chicken Cuts & Cooking Methods

Whole Bird (Roasting)

Best for: Roasting at 400-425¬∞F. Renders fat, cooks evenly. The cavity traps heat for faster cooking.

Breasts (Quick Cooking)

Best for: Pan-searing, grilling. Cook just until opaque (160¬∞F). Over-cooking makes them dry. Use for mild dishes.

Thighs (Forgiving)

Best for: Braising, stewing, roasting. Higher fat content means they stay juicy longer. Can handle bold flavors and longer cooking.

Wings (Stock & Crispy)

Best for: Stock (rich collagen), or crispy wings. Not much meat but lots of flavor from cartilage and skin.

Selection & Storage

What to Look For

Color: Should be pale pink to golden (not white or gray). Color indicates diet and freshness.

Smell: Fresh chicken smells mild. Any ammonia smell = old. Don't buy.

Skin: Taut and moist, not slimy or sticky.

Storage

Fresh: Use within 2 days of purchase. Store on the bottom shelf of the fridge (prevents drips onto other foods).

Frozen: Lasts 9-12 months. Thaw overnight in refrigerator, not on counter.
